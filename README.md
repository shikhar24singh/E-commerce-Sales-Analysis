# E-commerce-Sales-Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes sales performance across 1,000 products from multiple e-commerce categories using PostgreSQL, Python, and Power BI. The objective is to uncover revenue-driving categories, identify seasonal sales trends, evaluate product performance, and generate actionable business insights through data analysis and visualization.

## Problem Statement

E-commerce businesses generate large volumes of transactional data, making it difficult to identify key revenue drivers and growth opportunities. This project aims to transform raw sales data into meaningful insights that support data-driven decision-making.

## Dataset

The dataset contains:

* 1,000 Products
* 7 Product Categories
* Product Pricing Information
* Customer Review Scores & Counts
* Monthly Sales Data (12 Months)

### Features

* Product ID
* Product Name
* Product Category
* Price
* Review Score
* Review Count
* Monthly Sales (Jan–Dec)

## Key Insights

* Books emerged as the highest revenue-generating category.
* October recorded the highest sales volume.
* Revenue is concentrated among a small group of products.
* Customer ratings showed minimal correlation with sales performance.
* Home & Kitchen generated the lowest revenue contribution.

## Business Recommendations

* Increase investment in Books and Sports categories.
* Improve inventory planning before peak sales periods.
* Promote top-performing products through targeted campaigns.
* Expand successful product lines.
* Collect customer-level behavioral data for future predictive analytics.
